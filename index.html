About Me

I graduated with distinction with a Bachelor's degree in Mathematics from Saint Petersburg State University's Faculty of Mathematics and Computer Science. My undergraduate thesis, completed under the supervision of Prof. Serge Yagunov, investigated homological stability for general linear groups.

This fall, I will begin a Master's in Mathematics at the University of Münster. I plan to deepen my exploration of K-theory, homotopy theory, and group homology—broadly speaking, I am fascinated by areas where algebra intersects with topology and number theory.

Outside of mathematics, I have a wide range of passions, including theater, cinema, football, chess, and linguistics.

Research Experience

  • Homology stability for linear groups

    2025 | Saint Petersburg State University

    My bachelor's thesis, implementing and developing Professor S. Yagunov's idea for a simplified proof of (rational) homology stability for linear groups and related questions. A preprint is in preparation.

  • Torsion group of elliptic curve and volume numbers

    2024 | Saint Petersburg State University

    My 3rd-year course work, applying the machinery of elliptic curves to partially solve an arithmetic problem. The work was recognized in the Yadro coursework competition.

  • Binomial sums modulo prime number

    2023 | Saint Petersburg State University

    My first course work. Experimentally, very remarkable congruences were found, linking certain binomial sums to the number of points over a finite field of specific surfaces. Some of them are proven in the work, but some hypotheses, and the entire phenomenon, remain open. I would be very happy to share this project; I myself sometimes try to find its reflections in unexpected places.

  • Substructural searching in molecular database

    March 2022 - August 2022 | Quantori

    My (so far) only applied project: graphs, machine learning. I now remember it all only superficially, but it was interesting.

  • Knots, braids, and knotted surface transformations

    July 2022 - August 2022 | Mathematical Center in Akademgorodok

    An immersion into the world of braids, knot theory, and low-dimensional topology. I remember falling asleep at night starting to think about an object, which I learned by the next morning already existed—Brunnian braids.

  • Maximal advancement in the project problems about self-avoiding wandering

    May 2021 | Sirius Science Center

    My first research project. As a school student, we delved into a paper by Fields Medalist Stanislav Smirnov and reproved some of the theorems in his work.
